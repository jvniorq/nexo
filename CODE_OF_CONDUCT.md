# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Nexo aims to be a welcoming project where people can discuss product design, accessibility, privacy, engineering, and family organization respectfully. Everyone participating in this repository is expected to help create a safe and constructive environment.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Assume good intent while remaining accountable for impact.
- Welcome different backgrounds, abilities, identities, and levels of experience.
- Give actionable feedback and accept correction gracefully.
- Protect private information.
- Keep discussions relevant to the project.
- Credit ideas and contributions appropriately.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or unwanted attention
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or repeated bad-faith argument
- Publishing another person's private information
- Sharing credentials, household data, or sensitive reports
- Threats, incitement, or encouragement of harm
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional community

## Scope

This policy applies to repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, commits, and other public or private project spaces. It also applies when someone officially represents the project elsewhere.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner. Do not include sensitive information in a public issue. Reports will be reviewed fairly and in context. Possible responses include a private warning, removal of content, temporary participation limits, or a permanent ban.

The repository owner is responsible for interpreting and enforcing this policy and may remove contributions or comments that do not align with it.

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by widely used open-source community standards and is adapted for Nexo's current early stage.
