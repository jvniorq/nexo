# Product Vision

## One sentence

Nexo helps people organize their personal lives and coordinate their households from one calm, connected place without confusing private information with shared responsibilities.

## Product promise

Nexo should reduce the effort required to remember, plan, buy, store, and coordinate. It should feel useful on an ordinary Tuesday, not only during a major project or crisis.

## Who it is for

- Individuals who want a coherent personal organization system
- Couples coordinating time, shopping, and home responsibilities
- Families with adults and younger members
- People sharing a home
- Relatives or caregivers coordinating practical support

A person must be able to use Nexo without joining a household.

## Core spaces

### Personal space

Tasks, events, notes, habits, reminders, and information that remain private unless explicitly shared.

### Household space

Shopping, inventory, expiration dates, family events, responsibilities, and notes shared with selected household members.

Every item should have an owner, visibility, status, and optional responsible person.

## Product pillars

### Connected organization

Features should form useful flows instead of isolated modules. Shopping can feed inventory, inventory can trigger expiration reminders, notes can become actions, and tasks can become calendar blocks.

### Calm interaction

The interface should prioritize the next useful action without presenting every item as urgent. Language must remain neutral and supportive.

### Explicit privacy

Private and household contexts must always be visible before saving or sharing. The application should not expose personal activity through household feeds.

### Cross-platform continuity

Mobile should excel at capture, reminders, and shopping. Desktop should excel at planning, reviewing, keyboard interaction, drag and drop, and multi-column layouts.

### Offline confidence

Creating, editing, and completing essential items should remain possible without a network connection. Synchronization should happen later and communicate conflicts clearly.

## Primary navigation

1. **Today** — daily priorities, events, reminders, expirations, and household context
2. **Plan** — tasks, calendar, routines, recurrence, and focus
3. **Home** — shopping, inventory, expirations, members, and responsibilities
4. **Notes** — personal and shared knowledge
5. **Profile** — households, permissions, appearance, notifications, privacy, and export

## Experience principles

- System theme by default, with manual light and dark options
- Motion between 180 and 320 milliseconds for common transitions
- Reduced-motion support
- Minimum comfortable touch targets
- Color never used as the only status signal
- Progressive disclosure for advanced fields
- Undo for common destructive or state-changing actions
- Notifications requested only when their value is clear
- No streak punishment or family productivity rankings

## Initial MVP

The first useful release should include:

- Today dashboard
- Tasks and subtasks
- Basic calendar
- Shared shopping lists
- Household inventory
- Expiration reminders
- Notes
- Local notifications
- Offline local storage
- Adaptive mobile and desktop layouts
- Light, dark, and system appearance

Authentication, household synchronization, OCR, barcode scanning, voice capture, and AI-assisted interpretation can follow after the local product experience is validated.

## Success signals

Early validation should focus on behavior rather than vanity metrics:

- People return to the Today screen
- Household shopping lists prevent duplicate purchases
- Expiration reminders lead to useful action
- Users understand what is private and what is shared
- Capture feels fast on mobile
- Weekly planning feels comfortable on desktop
- Notification settings prevent overload
